Title: Freshman Looking for Advice on Everything

Category: Freshman Success, Campus Life, and Student Advice

Overview:

Incoming freshmen shared questions about what to expect at George Mason University. Current students responded with advice about academics, campus life, housing, dining, social life, health, safety, and time management.

Academic Success

- Do not underestimate college courses during your first semester.
- Read and regularly review every course syllabus.
- Many professors expect students to track assignments independently.
- Time management is one of the most important skills for success in college.
- Avoid procrastination and start assignments as early as possible.
- Stay aware of important deadlines, including add/drop dates and tuition refund deadlines.
- Attend office hours when struggling in a class.
- If a class is going poorly, evaluate options early rather than waiting until the end of the semester.
- Balance difficult and easier classes when building a schedule.
- Four classes per semester is generally considered manageable for most freshmen.

Professor and Course Selection

- Use Rate My Professors when selecting courses and instructors.
- Research professors before registering for classes.
- Student experiences can vary, so reviews should be considered alongside other information.

Textbooks and Costs

- Avoid purchasing textbooks before the first day of class unless absolutely necessary.
- Many required books are rarely used.
- Students often search for less expensive options online.
- Campus stores are generally considered expensive.
- Students recommend minimizing unnecessary spending on campus.

Campus Involvement and Social Life

- Join clubs and student organizations early.
- Consider pursuing leadership positions within organizations.
- Clubs provide opportunities to make friends, build leadership skills, and strengthen resumes.
- Some organizations offer scholarships and networking opportunities.
- Building friendships requires initiative and consistent effort.
- Many students recommend prioritizing social connections during the first year.

Housing and Roommates

- Random roommate assignments can produce very different experiences.
- Students should communicate expectations and boundaries with roommates early.
- If a roommate situation becomes unhealthy, students can request a room change.
- Students with qualifying disabilities may be eligible for housing accommodations.
- Dorm rooms are often small, so students should avoid bringing unnecessary items.

Dining and Food

- Student opinions on dining halls vary considerably.
- Southside Dining Hall received mixed reviews.
- Some students appreciated the variety of food options available.
- Others reported inconsistent quality and occasional food safety concerns.
- Ike’s Dining Hall was described as more consistently average.
- Students recommend monitoring food quality and avoiding food that appears unsafe.

Health and Wellness

- Prioritize sleep, nutrition, hydration, and personal hygiene.
- College students often struggle with maintaining healthy routines.
- Students recommended eating fruits and vegetables regularly.
- Campus recreation facilities, including gyms and pools, are valuable resources.
- Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) provides mental health support for students.

Safety

- Campus is generally considered safe.
- Students should still exercise caution when walking alone late at night.
- Avoid unnecessary risks and remain aware of surroundings.
- Students are advised not to engage with groups attempting to provoke confrontations on campus.

Transportation and Parking

- Parking can be difficult and expensive.
- Students should carefully consider whether bringing a vehicle is necessary.
- Walking between classes can take significant time.
- Students should learn campus routes before classes begin.
- Weather conditions can affect travel time, so carrying an umbrella is recommended.

Technology and Academic Integrity

- Students should take the Honor Code seriously.
- Cheating, plagiarism, and unauthorized collaboration can result in severe academic penalties.
- Students should understand academic integrity policies before submitting coursework.

Career and Long-Term Planning

- Students should think seriously about potential majors and career goals.
- Career decisions should be based on interests, abilities, and long-term goals rather than external pressure.
- Students should remain realistic about educational and career requirements in their chosen field.

Additional Student Tips

- Visit campus before classes begin and become familiar with building locations.
- Use study rooms and library spaces when needing a quiet environment.
- Take advantage of university resources while they are available.
- Explore Washington, D.C. and Northern Virginia using university transportation options.
- Learn to balance academics, recreation, social life, and personal responsibilities.

Key Themes:

1. Time management is critical for academic success.
2. Avoid procrastination and start assignments early.
3. Join clubs and organizations to build friendships and professional skills.
4. Maintain healthy habits and prioritize self-care.
5. Research professors and course requirements before registration.
6. Understand the Honor Code and academic integrity expectations.
7. College success requires both academic effort and personal responsibility.
8. Taking advantage of campus resources can significantly improve the student experience.
