Title: Hidden Spots Around Campus

Category: Campus Life and Student Recommendations

Overview:

Students discussed lesser-known locations around George Mason University where people can relax, think, spend time alone, or enjoy quiet surroundings away from crowded study areas and common campus gathering spaces.

Student Recommendations:

Sword in the Stone Trail

Located near the Engineering Building on the way toward Lot A.
A small footbridge leads to a trail.
The trail contains a sword embedded in a chained boulder.
Students described it as a unique and interesting hidden location.

Aquia Building Top Floor

Located at the back of the Aquia Building.
Accessible using the rear staircase facing away from the main campus.
Features tables and large windows overlooking the woods.
Usually quiet and rarely occupied except for occasional staff lunches.
Recommended as a peaceful place to sit and reflect.

Mason Pond Hidden Area

Located near the banks of Mason Pond.
Contains an unusual statue and an abandoned raft or boat.
Students described it as a hidden "Easter egg" location on campus.

Blue Ridge to Southside Walkway

The path connecting Blue Ridge Hall and Southside contains several secluded benches.
Students reported that these areas are rarely crowded.
Recommended for relaxation and quiet reflection.

Johnson Center Lower Level

Located on the ground floor of the Johnson Center beyond the restrooms.
Contains an open carpeted space with numerous power outlets.
Usually occupied by only a few students at a time.
Considered one of the quieter indoor spaces on campus.

Innovation Hall Garden Trail

Located beside Innovation Hall.
Features a small trail, garden area, and bench.
Students described it as a peaceful location away from heavy foot traffic.

Peterson Hall Outdoor Area

Outdoor seating area near Peterson Hall.
Frequently described as quiet and uncrowded.
Suitable for studying, reading, or relaxing outdoors.

Civil War Encampment Trail

Accessible from a trail near one of the larger parking lots close to the Mason Global Center parking deck.
Contains the remains of a Civil War encampment discovered on university property.
Students reported that the area is rarely visited.
Recommended for privacy, walking, and quiet reflection.

Key Themes:

Many students seek locations on campus that are quiet but not necessarily traditional study spaces.
Hidden outdoor locations are often preferred for relaxation and reflection.
Several lesser-known areas around GMU offer privacy despite the university's large student population.
Students frequently share informal knowledge about unique campus locations that are not highlighted in official university resources.
